Andrés Occhipinti Liberman

I am an assistant professor at ICAI School of Engineering, Department of Telematics and Computer Science, Comillas Pontifical University (Madrid, Spain). I teach courses in natural language processing, foundations of AI, and algorithms. My research focuses on learning dynamics models for automated planning. Currently, I am also exploring ways to improve the reasoning and planning abilities of LLMs. I am interested as well in theory-of-mind reasoning, epistemic planning and epistemic logics, which are areas I have previously worked on.

Before joining Comillas University, I worked as a postdoc in the Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning Group at Universitat Pompeu Fabra (Barcelona, Spain), as a member of the EU project Representation Learning for Planning (RLeap). Prior to that, I was an external lecturer in the Department of Applied Mathematics and Computer Science at the Technical University of Denmark (DTU), where I also earned my Ph.D. in artificial intelligence and logic. I also hold an M.Sc. in Logic from the Institute for Logic, Language and Computation (ILLC) at the University of Amsterdam.

Keywords: artificial intelligence, natural language processing, planning, logic, learning symbolic representations, learning world models.
